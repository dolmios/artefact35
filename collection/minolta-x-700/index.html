DESCRIPTION

The Minolta X-700 is a 35mm single-lens reflex film camera introduced by Minolta in 1981. It was the top model of their final manual-focus SLR series.The X-700 was aimed to appeal to the widest range of photographers possible. Its easy to use fully automated Program mode could turn it into a point-and-shoot that anybody could use, but its wide array of advanced features and available accessories and lenses made it appealing to professionals alike.
